Guide for Canadians Buying Real Estate Property in Belize

Buying property in Belize as a Canadian can be a fantastic option, whether for passive income or to enjoy the Caribbean lifestyle. Here's how to do it and what you need to know.

The country of Belize might be tiny, but it packs a punch when it comes to real estate appeal. There are many reasons why Belize is a world-class living destination. It has tropical weather, swoon-worthy beaches, ancient ruins, cheap properties, and no restrictions on foreign property buys.

If you're planning to buy property in Belize, there are many factors to consider, so it's wise to research and understand all the twists involved.

Why buy property in Belize?

Belize is a Caribbean country. Located on the northeastern coast of Central America, it's bordered by Mexico (north), the Caribbean Sea (east), and Guatemala (west+south). 

Canada-Belize relations are generally good, and Belize is home to some Canadians. Here are some excellent reasons for joining the ex-pat community in Belize.

  • More affordable options than Canada. According to Forbes¹, Canada's real estate market is currently overpriced. You might find better luck fulfilling your property ownership dreams by venturing into Belize's emerging market, which offers many better value options.
  • The property tax is cheap. You also get the benefit of lower property taxes. Land taxes range from 1% to 1.5%, for perspective².
  • No red tape. Belize allows Canadian citizens to buy property there. The process is remarkably straightforward.
  • The first language is English. Belize operates as a member of the British Commonwealth and the British monarchy heads the state, so English is the primary language.
  • The weather is sunny. If you hate Canada's brutal winters, owning a second home in Belize allows you to become a "snowbird" ex-pat. As a snowbird, you can stay home when the weather is good and trade the winters for sunny weather in Belize to get the best of both worlds.

5 essential steps to purchasing property in Belize

1. Do your research

Buying property in Belize could be one of your biggest investments. Hence, it's crucial to research the property market – its past trends, forecasts and hotspots. This is the first step in deciding where to invest in real estate.

2. Take a trip to Belize

Generally, it's good to put eyeballs on the property before buying. To be thorough, we recommend visiting Belize and touring areas like Ambergris Caye, San Ignacio, San Pedro, Corozal, Placencia and to get a feel of the whole country. It's best to use an accredited broker since the real estate industry in Belize is unregulated.

3. Understand the laws

A reputable lawyer is an important factor in helping your purchase go smoothly. They'll help you understand the basics of ownership, such as the tax implications and obligations that come with purchasing international property. They'll also handle the legal aspect of the buying process and ensure a satisfactory contract overall. 

4. Calculate the costs

The cost of buying property overseas typically includes mortgage fees, money transfer fees, legal fees, insurance costs, service fees and utility costs. Factor in all relevant costs to prepare a workable budget.

5. Transfer the payment

Once all the paperwork is in order, it's time to make the international money transfer.  Using a specialist transfer service is better to avoid poor exchange rates and high bank transfer fees.

Why exchange rates matter?

Buy property in the Belize – FAQs

What are some key details to know before buying property in Belize?

Belize generally has friendly locals that welcome foreigners, so don't hesitate to understand the Belizian culture. 

However, remember Belize is a third-world country. Although it has plenty of natural beauty, the available luxuries might not compare to what you're used to in Canada.

How do I get a mortgage on a Belize property?

A mortgage from a Belizean financial institution can be a viable option if you're a legal resident. Otherwise, you'll need to obtain a mortgage with a Canadian lender or an international bank that considers Canadian applications.
